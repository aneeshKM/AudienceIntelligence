“Trending Audience” Builder (Public Wikimedia)
The Scenario
Companies constantly try to identify cultural or market trends early to package them into high-value audience
segments for brands. Manual analysis is too slow to catch viral trends. Your job is to build a prototype of an
autonomous Audience Trend Miner that transforms real-time search traffic into actionable target
demographics.
What is an audience segment?
In advertising and marketing, an audience segment is a group of people bundled together because
they share a trait, interest, behavior, or intent that makes them worth targeting as a unit. For example,
“new parents,” “budget-conscious travelers,” or “people upgrading their home for energy efficiency.”
Brands buy access to segments rather than to individuals: instead of advertising to everyone, they
pay to reach the specific group most likely to care about their product.
A good segment is (1) coherent — the members genuinely belong together; (2) sizable enough to be
worth a campaign; and (3) commercially meaningful — you can name the kinds of brands that would
pay to reach it. Your job in this project is to automatically discover such segments from what the
public is suddenly reading on Wikipedia, and to describe each one as a marketer would.
Your Task
Using the free, public Wikipedia Pageviews API, fetch the top trending articles for the week. Your agentic
pipeline must analyze this raw list, filter out non-commercial noise (e.g., random historical figures or breaking
global news tragedies), and synthesize the remaining trends into a structured "Emerging Audience Portfolio."
For each target audience discovered, the engine must dynamically generate:
● Audience Name: A catchy, market-friendly taxonomy name.
● Audience Description: A brief narrative detailing why this group is suddenly trending based on
Wikipedia traffic signals.
● Estimated Size Index: A normalized calculation relative to total trending traffic.
● Potential Buying Power: A smart qualitative LLM assessment of the group’s high/medium/low
commercial value and the types of brands that should target them that yields results a human editor
would give thumbs up to
The Public Data & Technical Guardrails
● Data Source: Free Wikipedia Pageviews API (unauthenticated, raw JSON return).
● The Constraint: The candidate must write a clean custom grouping/clustering loop (via embeddings or
LLM state architecture) to merge disparate trending topics (e.g., merging pages about "Solar Panels,"
"Heat Pumps," and "Electric Bikes" into an “Eco-Home Modernizers” audience).
